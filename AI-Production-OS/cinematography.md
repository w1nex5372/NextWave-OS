# Cinematography

Use realistic documentary camera language. Make every shot feel filmed, not generated.

## Default Camera Style

- Aspect ratio: 9:16 vertical.
- Lens feel: 35mm to 70mm documentary realism.
- Depth of field: shallow to medium, never excessive blur.
- Lighting: soft key light, practical office lights, controlled highlights.
- Camera movement: slow, purposeful, stable.
- Color: clean neutral grade, realistic skin tones, subtle contrast.

## Shot Types

- Establishing shot: shows the block environment.
- Medium shot: people working or reacting.
- Closeup: face, hand, laptop, phone, notebook, chart, dashboard.
- Over-the-shoulder: shows a person viewing a screen or document.
- Detail insert: product UI, graph, keyboard, meeting note, calendar, investor deck.
- Slow push-in: builds importance.
- Slow pull-back: reveals scale.
- Lateral slide: creates premium motion.
- Rack focus: shifts attention between person and object.

## Pacing Defaults

- Hook shots: 1.2-2.0 seconds.
- Explanation shots: 2.0-3.5 seconds.
- Evidence shots: 2.5-4.0 seconds.
- Emotional beat shots: 3.0-4.5 seconds.
- Outro shot: 2.0-3.0 seconds.

## Camera Direction Formula

Each shot must include:

```text
Camera: [shot size], [lens feel], [movement], [focus target], [lighting note], [duration].
```

Example:

```text
Camera: medium closeup, 50mm documentary lens feel, slow push-in, focus on founder's face then laptop reflection, soft office window light, 2.5 seconds.
```

## Video Tool Motion Rules

For Hailuo, Kling, Runway, Pika, and Minimax:

- Ask for one camera move only.
- Keep motion subtle.
- Preserve identity, room, wardrobe, props, and lighting.
- Avoid morphing, new objects, extra fingers, unreadable text, and hallucinated screens.
- Use "no scene change" and "no new environment" in every same-block shot prompt.
