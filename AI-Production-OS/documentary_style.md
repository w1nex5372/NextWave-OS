# Documentary Style

Use this style for every production package unless the producer gives a different brand direction.

## Visual Identity

- Premium business documentary.
- Realistic corporate environments.
- Modern SaaS and AI startup aesthetic.
- Clean offices, boardrooms, labs, product spaces, data centers, conference rooms, and city business districts.
- Human-centered scenes: founders, operators, analysts, engineers, customers, executives, teams.
- Cinematic but believable lighting.
- Neutral color base with controlled accents.

## Approved Looks

- Netflix business documentary.
- Apple product launch realism.
- Google workspace clarity.
- Microsoft enterprise polish.
- Bloomberg-style business credibility.
- Founder documentary interviews.
- Real office strategy sessions.
- Product workflow closeups.
- Investor meeting atmosphere.

## Banned Looks

Do not use:

- Cyberpunk.
- Neon sci-fi cityscapes.
- Random robots.
- Fake AI brains.
- Floating hologram overload.
- Glowing humanoid assistants.
- Broken or unreadable AI text on screens.
- Cheap TikTok effects.
- Random glitch overlays.
- Overly dramatic smoke, lasers, or dystopian lighting.
- Inconsistent environments inside one block.

## Environment Rules

- Each block gets one environment through its master image.
- Same block means same room, lighting, people, props, time of day, and visual tone.
- New block may change environment only if the story beat changes.
- Screens may show abstract dashboards, charts, or product UI, but no readable fake text unless exact text is supplied.

## People Rules

- People must look like real professionals.
- Clothing: modern business casual, founder casual, product team attire, analyst attire, executive attire.
- Avoid exaggerated emotions.
- Use restrained documentary expressions: focus, tension, curiosity, confidence, concern.

## Screen Content Rules

Use:

- Clean dashboards.
- Realistic charts.
- Abstract analytics.
- Product workflow shapes.
- Spreadsheet-like grids without readable nonsense text.

Avoid:

- Gibberish text.
- Fake brand names.
- Long readable UI copy.
- Random code walls.
