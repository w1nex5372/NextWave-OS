# Example Brief - Video 01

## Input Script

Use `scripts/video_01_script.md`.

## Direction

- Target length: 60 seconds.
- Format: 9:16.
- Tone: sharp, credible, premium, business documentary.
- Avatar role: the example brief explicitly assigns the script's Hook to Avatar Intro and CTA to Avatar Outro.
- Presenter boundary: avatar footage is limited to the explicitly assigned intro and outro; all five documentary blocks exclude the presenter.
- Visual approach: corporate workers establish the fear world; an individual builder appears only when the narration shifts toward agents and founder leverage.
- Cinematic language: human-first Netflix business documentary with restrained Apple clarity.
- Lens base: 35mm environmental coverage, 50mm human explanation, 85mm reactions, 100mm macro details.
- Camera logic: locked for truth, push for realization, pull for scale, rack focus for attention shifts.
- Composition: documentary asymmetry, layered foregrounds, negative space during fear, stable framing at payoff.
- Lighting arc: cold morning fear, neutral thinking, blue focus, amber confidence, golden opportunity.
- B-roll strategy: real desks, coffee, paper maps, phones, whiteboards, human reactions; screens remain secondary.
- Visual rhythm: wide -> medium -> close -> detail -> reaction -> wide.

## Block Strategy

- Block 1: Fear. `VISUAL_SUBJECT`: corporate employees reacting to AI job-loss headlines and workplace pressure; no founder apartment or solution imagery.
- Block 2: Thinking. `VISUAL_SUBJECT`: an individual builder in a modest home office testing isolated tasks and beginning notebook planning; no completed system.
- Block 3: Building. `VISUAL_SUBJECT`: same founder and workspace, with whiteboard, cards, and visible connections introduced.
- Block 4: Operations. `VISUAL_SUBJECT`: same founder and evolved workspace, with calendar, active tasks, laptop workflow, and parcels introduced by the story.
- Block 5: Momentum. `VISUAL_SUBJECT`: same founder in the now-organized working business, with orders, systems, and visible maturity supported by the payoff.

## Cinematic QA

- Reject dashboard-led sequences.
- Reject any presenter footage inside Blocks 1-5.
- Reject any master image that depicts the narrator instead of its declared `VISUAL_SUBJECT`.
- Reject any master image reused across blocks.
- Reject future-block props or maturity appearing early.
- Require founder identity, wardrobe, architecture, and accumulated props to remain continuous.
- Reject repeated lenses or moves more than twice.
- Require a human reaction after each major claim.
- Require a breathing moment before the payoff.
- Require a complete Editor Assistant timeline.
