# Sound Design

Purpose: make generated visuals feel physically present and editorially connected.

## SFX Library

| Sound | Story Function | Layering Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Typing | Work rhythm, concentration | Low level under room tone; avoid constant loops |
| Mouse | Precision, choice | Single click on motivated action |
| Coffee | Ritual, fatigue, intimacy | Cup set-down, pour, spoon; use one clear detail |
| Chair | Human presence, tension | Soft shift or scrape during reaction |
| Footsteps | Momentum, approach, location | Match surface and perspective |
| Phone | Pressure, communication | Vibration, pickup, lock; avoid loud generic rings |
| Notification | Accumulation or urgency | One or short sequence; never social-media spam |
| Paper | Planning, evidence, transition | Page turn, slide, fold, sticky note |
| Pen | Thought and decision | Mark, tap, cap; sync to hand detail |
| Marker | Strategy and explanation | Cap click, whiteboard stroke |
| Keyboard | Execution and workflow | Distinct key texture; separate from typing bed |
| Ambience | Establish place | Build location-specific bed before spot effects |
| City | Scale and movement | Traffic wash, distant voices, crossing signal |
| Office | Credibility and space | HVAC, distant conversation, chairs, printers |
| Room Tone | Continuity and silence | Maintain under edits and music drops |
| Drone | Aerial scale | Wind bed only if perspective supports it |
| Wind | Exposure, transition, exterior space | Keep subtle and directional |
| Door | Arrival, departure, access | Latch, hinge, close; use as transition punctuation |

## Layering Order

1. Dialogue.
2. Room tone or ambience.
3. Music.
4. Motivated spot SFX.
5. Transition accent.

## Mixing Rules

- Keep dialogue intelligible at all times.
- Use room tone under silence to avoid digital emptiness.
- Use no more than one or two spot effects per shot.
- Match perspective: close sounds are detailed; wide sounds are diffuse.
- Avoid generic cinematic booms on minor claims.
- Use SFX to confirm physical action, not to decorate captions.
