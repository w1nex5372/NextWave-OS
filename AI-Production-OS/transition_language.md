# Transition Language

Purpose: choose transitions from story continuity rather than decoration.

| Transition | Purpose | Use | Do Not Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cut | Preserve clarity and pace | Most documentary edits | When a motivated audio or visual bridge is stronger |
| Match Cut | Connect shape, action, gaze, or idea | Paper-to-screen, hand-to-hand, room-to-room | Without a visible or conceptual match |
| J-Cut | Let next scene audio lead | Anticipation, smooth location change | When incoming sound confuses the current image |
| L-Cut | Let current audio continue over next image | Reflection, continuity, voice-led B-roll | When dialogue source must remain visible |
| Whip | Compress time with energy | Rare high-momentum transitions | Serious interviews, emotional pauses |
| Fade | Signal gentle passage or conclusion | Soft ending, time passage | Routine shot changes |
| Dip To Black | Mark a major reset or finality | Chapter break, ending, emotional rupture | Multiple times in short-form video |
| Light Leak | Suggest memory or organic time shift | Rare warm transition | Corporate clarity, tension, evidence |
| Speed Ramp | Emphasize physical acceleration | Walking, manufacturing, travel | Office typing or emotional dialogue |
| Flash | Represent camera, press, memory shock | Rare archival or event beat | General emphasis or cheap impact |
| Hold Frame | Let information or emotion land | Final expression, evidence, CTA | When subject motion looks awkward frozen |
| Cross Dissolve | Blend time, memory, or similarity | Reflection, long passage | Fast modern business pacing without reason |

## Transition Rules

- Default to a cut.
- Use match cuts for visual logic.
- Use J-cuts and L-cuts to make edits feel authored and continuous.
- Use stylized transitions no more than once or twice per short video.
- Preserve motion direction and eye-line where possible.
- Do not use glitch transitions, random wipes, spin transitions, or flash spam.
