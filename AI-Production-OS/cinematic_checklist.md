# Cinematic Checklist

Purpose: provide the highest-level rejection gate for every production package.

Script authority overrides every cinematic preference in this checklist. Resolve cinematic failures by enriching the preserved shot, never by removing, merging, splitting, replacing, adding, reordering, or retiming script shots.

## Immediate Rejection Conditions

Reject and fix the package if:

- The hierarchy differs from Avatar Intro, Blocks 1-5, Avatar Outro.
- One documentary production block contains multiple script blocks or the entire story.
- A script block is split across production blocks.
- A master image covers or is reused across multiple script blocks.
- A block master image reveals future-story elements.
- Environment, props, lighting mood, current-character posture, or business maturity fails to evolve.
- A recurring character's identity or script-defined wardrobe breaks continuity after introduction.
- The visual world is not supported by the current block's script evidence.
- A founder replaces workers or corporate context before founder context is established.
- Objects teleport, disappear without motivation, or appear before introduction.
- Dashboard footage dominates the sequence.
- AI interfaces become the primary subject.
- A presenter appears in a documentary block without explicit script permission.
- The narrator becomes the visual focus instead of the narrated world.
- A documentary block substitutes presenter coverage for cinematic visual storytelling.
- The visuals fail to represent the concept described by the narration.
- The same camera movement repeats more than twice.
- The same lighting state repeats without emotional or continuity reason.
- The same lens repeats more than twice without a deliberate visual strategy.
- The same composition repeats more than twice.
- Emotion stagnates across a block.
- Technology becomes the protagonist.
- Story escalation is missing.
- The video has no visual payoff.
- The ending is weaker than the opening.
- The first frame is unclear, generic, or visually empty.
- The sequence has no breathing moment.
- B-roll feels generic or stock-like.
- Camera movement is unmotivated.
- Color changes are arbitrary.
- Music does not follow emotional progression.
- Sound design is decorative rather than physical.
- Transitions call attention to themselves without story purpose.

## Cinematic Pass

- [ ] A person, decision, or consequence anchors every block.
- [ ] Each script block maps to one production block in exact order.
- [ ] Each documentary block owns one unique master image.
- [ ] Environment and business maturity evolve without revealing future information.
- [ ] Every documentary block declares a `VISUAL_SUBJECT`, and its master image represents that world.
- [ ] Presenter footage is confined to explicitly scripted presenter scenes.
- [ ] Every shot has a clear story purpose.
- [ ] Shot type, lens, movement, composition, lighting, and color state are intentional.
- [ ] Visual scale changes naturally.
- [ ] Human reactions follow important information.
- [ ] Screens are motivated by human action.
- [ ] Master images look like real documentary photographs.
- [ ] The color script follows the emotional arc.
- [ ] Music and silence support the story.
- [ ] Sound effects create physical presence.
- [ ] The editor timeline is executable.
- [ ] The final payoff receives more visual space than the setup.
