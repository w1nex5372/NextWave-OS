# Camera Language

Purpose: define motivated camera movement for documentary scenes.

| Movement | Purpose | Emotion | Recommended Length | Use | Do Not Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Locked | Force observation | Honesty, tension | 2-6s | Reactions, silence, decisions | When active geography must be revealed |
| Tripod | Stable controlled coverage | Authority, trust | 2-7s | Interviews, executive scenes | To imitate energy |
| Handheld | Add human presence | Urgency, uncertainty | 2-6s | Real work, pressure, candid action | Polished product payoff |
| Slider Left | Reveal relationship left-to-right | Discovery, progression | 2-5s | Desk, person-to-object reveal | Without a reveal |
| Slider Right | Reverse reveal or release | Resolution, orientation | 2-5s | Return movement, room detail | Repeated after slider left without purpose |
| Push | Increase importance or intimacy | Focus, tension | 2-6s | Realization, key line, decision | Every talking shot |
| Pull | Reveal scale or isolation | Consequence, relief | 3-7s | Payoff, loneliness, room reveal | When wider frame adds nothing |
| Tilt | Connect vertical information | Discovery, status | 1-4s | Person to building, hand to face | Decorative movement |
| Pan | Follow attention or reveal | Orientation, curiosity | 2-5s | Gaze, action, room relationship | Fast random scanning |
| Orbit | Show relationship around subject | Transformation, energy | 3-6s | Major payoff, product craft | Routine documentary coverage |
| Crane | Reveal scale from above/below | Grandeur, consequence | 4-8s | Factory, construction, city | Small human scenes |
| Dolly | Smooth physical approach or retreat | Precision, inevitability | 3-7s | Controlled tension, major reveal | When a slider or locked shot is enough |
| Gimbal Walk | Follow purposeful travel | Momentum, access | 3-8s | Founder walking, operations, city | Stationary conversation |
| Rack Focus | Shift narrative attention | Realization, connection | 1-4s | Person-to-object, foreground-to-background | Without two meaningful subjects |
| Parallax | Reveal depth and relationship | Discovery, polish | 2-5s | Foreground objects, layered offices | Flat spaces with no depth |
| Static | Create breathing space | Reflection, confidence | 2-7s | Emotional pause, aftermath | During unclear action |
| Macro Push | Intensify a meaningful detail | Precision, tension | 1-3s | Pen, eye, switch, material | Random object beauty |
| Micro Drift | Simulate quiet documentary presence | Intimacy, unease | 2-5s | Close human observation | When true locked stability is needed |

## Automatic Movement Rules

- Prefer locked or static if no movement is motivated.
- Use one movement per image-to-video prompt.
- Do not repeat the same movement more than twice in succession.
- Movement direction should support edit direction and screen geography.
- Fast movement requires a real story reason.
