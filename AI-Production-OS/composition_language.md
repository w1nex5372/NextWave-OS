# Composition Language

Purpose: shape attention and emotion inside each frame.

| Rule | Purpose | Psychological Effect | Business Documentary Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rule Of Thirds | Create natural balance | Accessible, observational | Interviews, founder at desk, walking coverage |
| Negative Space | Show pressure, possibility, or isolation | Vulnerability, anticipation | Founder alone, market uncertainty, text placement |
| Leading Lines | Direct attention through environment | Momentum, inevitability | Hallways, desks, factory lines, city streets |
| Symmetry | Express control, scale, or systems | Authority, precision | Boardroom, architecture, payoff; use selectively |
| Foreground Blur | Add depth and observation | Intimacy, candid access | Interview through objects, office activity |
| Background Layering | Make environment informative | Credibility, complexity | Real offices, workshops, operations floors |
| Frame Within Frame | Isolate subject inside context | Pressure, focus, privacy | Doorways, windows, glass offices |
| Head Room | Preserve natural human framing | Comfort, professionalism | Avatar, interview, conversation |
| Eye Room | Give gaze somewhere to travel | Expectation, thought | Profile, OTS, reactions |
| Subject Isolation | Prioritize one person or choice | Importance, intimacy | Key claim, decision, emotional turn |
| Depth | Separate foreground, subject, background | Immersion, realism | Founder work, city, factory, team scenes |
| Documentary Asymmetry | Avoid artificial perfection | Authenticity, tension | Desks, candid meetings, real work |

## Composition Rules

- Compose around the human action first.
- Keep screens secondary unless the screen contains essential evidence.
- Use asymmetry by default for observational B-roll.
- Use symmetry only for intentional control, authority, or payoff.
- Protect vertical safe zones for captions without making composition empty.
- Do not repeat the same composition more than twice in succession.
- Maintain eye-line and movement continuity across cuts.
