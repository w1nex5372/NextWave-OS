# Music Direction

Purpose: shape emotional progression without overpowering narration.

| Phase | Energy | Tempo | Instrumentation | Emotion | Music Drop | Silence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Opening | Low but immediate | 70-95 BPM | Low pulse, restrained piano, muted texture | Curiosity, tension | Before first major claim | Short silence after hook |
| Problem | Controlled pressure | 75-100 BPM | Low synth, soft percussion, bass pulse | Concern, urgency | On a human reaction | Use room tone for consequence |
| Tension | Rising but restrained | 85-110 BPM | Percussive pulse, strings, sub texture | Uncertainty, pressure | Before reveal | Use silence to make risk land |
| Discovery | Clear forward motion | 85-105 BPM | Warm keys, light pulse, subtle plucks | Insight, possibility | At the exact reframe line | Brief pause before mechanism |
| Momentum | Moderate-high | 95-120 BPM | Tight percussion, bass, rhythmic texture | Progress, control | Before final thesis | Rare; preserve flow |
| Victory | Warm and controlled | 85-110 BPM | Warm synth, piano, light strings | Confidence, achievement | Reduce layers under key line | Silence before final payoff if strong |
| Ending | Resolving | 65-95 BPM | Sustained warm tone, piano tail | Reflection, invitation | Fade after final word | Leave room tone or clean tail |

## Music Rules

- Dialogue remains primary.
- Music should evolve at emotional turns, not every cut.
- Avoid epic trailer music for ordinary business stories.
- Avoid cheerful corporate stock music.
- Use a maximum of one core cue plus stems for most short videos.
- Drop percussion before a major thesis or emotional reaction.
- Use silence as an intentional layer, not missing audio.
