# Shot Language

Purpose: provide the Production Director with a complete shot library.

## Selection Rule

Select shot type from story purpose and emotion first. Do not repeat the same shot type more than twice in succession.

| Shot Type | Purpose | Emotion | Camera Height | Movement | Typical Duration | Use | Do Not Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wide Establishing | Define place, scale, and isolation | Context, anticipation | Eye or slightly high | Locked, pull, slow pan | 2-5s | Start a location or reveal consequence | When location is already clear |
| Medium | Show person and action together | Connection, focus | Eye level | Locked, slide, push | 2-5s | Work, conversation, decisions | For tiny object details |
| Medium Close | Prioritize speaker while retaining context | Authority, intimacy | Eye level | Locked, subtle push | 2-4s | Avatar, founder explanation, key claim | Repeated for entire sequence |
| Close | Read face, hand, or decisive action | Emotion, pressure | Subject level | Locked, micro push | 2-4s | Reaction, realization, decision | Without a clear emotional beat |
| Extreme Close | Isolate a critical human detail | Tension, intensity | Detail level | Static, macro push | 1-3s | Eye, finger, pen pressure, breath | As decoration |
| Macro | Reveal texture or tiny mechanism | Precision, discovery | Object level | Macro push, locked | 1-3s | Product texture, ink, hardware | Faces or broad context |
| Insert | Clarify story information | Understanding | Object level | Locked, slide | 1-3s | Phone, notebook, document, clock | Unmotivated screen filler |
| Detail | Make work tactile and believable | Authenticity | Object level | Micro drift, rack focus | 1-4s | Coffee, cables, worn desk, notes | When no story information is added |
| POV | Put viewer inside a decision | Urgency, empathy | Character eye level | Handheld, locked | 1-4s | Reading, choosing, entering | When geography becomes confusing |
| OTS | Connect person to object or another person | Attention, participation | Shoulder level | Push, rack focus | 2-5s | Screens, whiteboards, conversations | If the shoulder blocks key action |
| Profile | Observe thought or conversation | Reflection, tension | Eye level | Locked, slider | 2-5s | Calls, listening, decision pauses | Direct authoritative delivery |
| Silhouette | Reduce identity to emotional shape | Isolation, transition | Eye or low | Locked, pull | 2-4s | Window reflection, late-night work | Routine office coverage |
| Reflection | Show divided thought or layered identity | Uncertainty, introspection | Eye level | Locked, micro drift | 2-4s | Glass, mirror, monitor reflection | When reflection feels artificial |
| Reveal | Introduce information through composition | Discovery, surprise | Depends on reveal | Slide, pull, pan | 2-5s | New scale, person, consequence | Without a meaningful reveal |
| Tracking | Follow purposeful movement | Momentum, urgency | Waist or eye | Gimbal, dolly | 3-7s | Walking, factory, office movement | Static emotional moments |
| Hand Detail | Show effort, hesitation, or control | Tension, craft | Hand level | Locked, macro push | 1-3s | Pen, keyboard, phone, notes | Generic typing montage |
| Object Detail | Establish evidence or meaning | Clarity, memory | Object level | Locked, slide | 1-3s | Contract, coffee, product, tool | Objects unrelated to narration |
| Environmental Detail | Make a place lived-in | Authenticity, mood | Object or eye | Locked, pan | 1-3s | Rain window, lamp, empty chair | As random atmosphere |
| Reaction Shot | Show meaning landing on a person | Empathy, realization | Eye level | Locked, micro push | 2-4s | After claim, reveal, decision | Before the cause is established |
| Cutaway | Bridge time or clarify context | Rhythm, orientation | Variable | Locked, short move | 1-3s | Hands, room, audience, street | To hide weak story structure |
| B-roll | Support narration with observed reality | Context, credibility | Variable | Motivated only | 2-5s | Evidence, work, place, consequence | Generic stock-like filler |

## Automatic Shot Rhythm

Default sequence:

```text
Wide -> Medium -> Close -> Detail -> Reaction -> Wide
```

Change the order when story logic requires it, but maintain variation in scale, distance, and emotional function.
