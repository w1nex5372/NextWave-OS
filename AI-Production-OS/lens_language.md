# Lens Language

Purpose: select lenses from story, space, and emotion rather than visual novelty.

| Lens | Field Of View | Compression | Emotion | Documentary Usage | Founder Usage | Business Usage | Automatic Choice |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 18mm | Very wide, immersive | Very low | Scale, instability, urgency | Tight spaces, dramatic environment | Rare room reveal | Factory, warehouse, architecture | Only when environment scale is essential |
| 24mm | Wide with strong depth | Low | Momentum, presence | Walkthroughs, active rooms | Founder moving through workspace | Operations floor, construction, city | Choose for movement through space |
| 35mm | Natural wide context | Moderate-low | Authenticity, observation | Establishing, environmental portraits | Desk, apartment, small office | Team work, meetings, real operations | Default for human plus environment |
| 50mm | Natural perspective | Moderate | Clarity, trust, focus | Interviews, medium coverage | Founder explanation, decision | Leadership, product discussion | Default for balanced human storytelling |
| 85mm | Tight and compressed | High | Intimacy, tension, confidence | Closeups, reactions | Founder emotion, key line | Executive reaction, consequence | Choose for emotional isolation |
| 100mm Macro | Very tight detail | High | Precision, tactility | Hands, objects, materials | Notebook, phone, hardware | Product, factory detail, evidence | Choose for meaningful tiny details |
| 135mm | Narrow, highly compressed | Very high | Distance, observation, pressure | Candid observation across space | Founder alone through doorway | Boardroom, city, factory observation | Choose for private or surveilled feeling |

## Lens Rules

- Do not change lenses without changing narrative purpose.
- Do not repeat the same lens more than twice unless continuity requires it.
- Use 35mm and 50mm as the documentary base.
- Use 85mm for emotion, not generic polish.
- Use macro only for details that carry story information.
- Avoid 18mm close to faces.
- In vertical format, protect faces and hands from edge distortion.
