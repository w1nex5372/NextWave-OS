# Motion Language

Purpose: control pacing, stillness, montage, and payoff across different video lengths.

## Script Authority

The script controls shot count, block count, timing, order, and pacing. Use this module to enrich motion inside preserved shots only. Length-specific ranges are references, never permission to add, remove, merge, split, or retime script shots.

## Core Timing Rules

- Meaningful visual change: every 5-7 seconds maximum.
- Hook change: every 1-2.5 seconds during the first 6-8 seconds.
- Standard documentary shot: 2-5 seconds.
- Emotional hold: 2-4 seconds.
- Establishing shot: 2-5 seconds.
- Fast montage insert: 0.5-1.5 seconds.
- Slow motion: use only when physical action or emotion benefits from emphasis.
- Silence: use for a major realization, reaction, or ending beat.

## Motion Types

### Cuts

Increase cut speed for urgency and reduce it for emotion. Never cut only to maintain activity.

### Slow Motion

Use at 70-85% speed for tactile actions, walking, reaction, or atmosphere. Avoid extreme slow motion for ordinary office behavior.

### Static Shots

Use for authority, discomfort, truth, and breathing room. At least one static or nearly static shot is recommended in videos over 45 seconds.

### Fast Montage

Use for accumulation, repeated pressure, process scale, or compressed time. Limit to 3-6 shots before returning to a human anchor.

### Breathing Moments

Use after the hook, before the thesis, or after the payoff. A breathing moment may be a static reaction, room tone, or sustained wide frame.

### Silence

Drop music briefly when a line changes the argument or an emotion lands. Preserve dialogue and room tone.

### Build-Up

Increase rhythm, visual scale, and movement toward the reveal. Do not increase all three at once unless the story reaches a true climax.

### Payoff

Reduce visual clutter, stabilize camera, warm color, and give the final idea room to land.

## Length-Specific Pacing

These are diagnostic references only. They do not override script-defined structure or counts.

### 30-Second Video

- 6-10 shots.
- Hook within first second.
- One primary emotional turn.
- One breathing beat of 0.5-1.5 seconds.
- Avoid more than one montage.

### 60-Second Video

- 12-18 shots.
- Five preserved script blocks.
- Two or three emotional turns.
- One static breathing shot.
- Build momentum through the middle and simplify at payoff.

### 90-Second Video

- 18-26 shots.
- Five preserved script blocks.
- Establish protagonist, pressure, mechanism, and consequence.
- Use two breathing moments.
- Allow wider establishing and reaction shots.
